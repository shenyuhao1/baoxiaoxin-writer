Emilio Vasquez

Retired chef. Learned C at 50. Still cooking.

Life

Born in 1949 in Puerto Garza, a fishing village on the coast of Isla Chiquita. My father was a fisherman, my mother sold fried plantains at the local market.

Left school at 12 to wash dishes at El Pelícano Azul, a portside kitchen where an old Argentine sailor taught me how to make seafood stew. At 19, I bought a bus ticket to São Paulo and spent ten years as a line cook at Cantina do Marinheiro, learning Portuguese-style seafood rice.

Returned home at 35 and opened my own restaurant, La Olla de Cobre, in the capital city of Santa Lucía. Ran it for 28 years. Closed the doors in 2020 when the pandemic hit, and retired.

Work History

  • 1961–1968 El Pelícano Azul Puerto Garza — Dishwasher → Junior Cook
  • 1969–1979 Cantina do Marinheiro São Paulo, Brazil — Line Cook
  • 1984–2020 La Olla de Cobre Santa Lucía — Owner & Head Chef
  • 2015–2017 TallerSoft S.A. Santa Lucía — Part-time C Programmer

How I Found Programming

At age 50, my son brought home an old laptop with Turbo C 2.0 installed. I opened it out of curiosity and never stopped. C reminded me of cooking — simple ingredients, everything depends on technique. No shortcuts.

At 69, I uploaded my first project to GitHub. It was a small tool to auto-type menu entries for restaurant back-of-house systems. That tool eventually became BaoXiaoXin Writer.

I build tiny, zero-dependency tools in pure C. No frameworks, no runtimes, no bloat. Just a well-seasoned pan and a sharp knife.

My Project

BaoXiaoXin Writer — a keyboard simulation tool for Windows. Paste text character-by-character where copy-paste is blocked. Full Unicode support. Built-in Q&A database with fuzzy search. 58KB, pure C, zero dependencies.

Download the latest release, put it in a folder with sqlite3.dll, and double-click. No installer. No runtime. Nothing to configure.
